Content

Vision

    Problem/context: In this digital Era people's connections are becoming superficial and shallow

    Statement/belief: It is necessary to drift away from an excessive self-focus and open our minds and hearts to others

    Desired Outcome: Only then we can revive the sense of community and togetherness and build a world of real connections

    “In a world where life gets busy, and connections become brief and shallow. We believe that only if we manage to look outside of ourselves to others. Only then we can build a caring world of real connections, where we can truly reveal ourselves backstage."

Values

  1. Honesty: Our brand stands for authenticity and comfort, and we can only reach that outcome with honesty. By prioritizing honesty, we are motivating people to engage in deeper and more genuine connections.
  2. Open-mindedness: We aim to create a safe environment, where everyone feels welcome regardless of their background, age, gender etc It is important that we stablish the bases needed for people to feel seen, so that they can share their sincere opinion without being or feeling judged.
  3. Care: We prioritise empathy and compassion for others. This value involves looking beyond ourselves to others and build meaningful connections, fostering a community where individuals can be authentic and reveal themselves and feel supported by sharing their opinions and thoughts for a common passion.
  4. Support: We aim to create a platform where people can be themselves and talk to others about their connection with theatre. It is important to have a sense of support within this community where everyone can share their experiences without being judged.

Persona

    Name: Evelyn

    Age: 56 years old

    Occupation: Librarian

    Education: Bachelor's degree in English Literature

    Personality: Friendly, curious, sociable, cat-person

    Marital Status: Married

    Bio: Evelyn works as a librarian for most of her career, fostering a love of literature and learning in her community. In her free time, she attends book club meetings and explores cultural events like theatre performances. Evelyn has noticed a decline in the quality of theatre productions in recent years, which has decreased her enthusiasm for attending shows. However, she remains hopeful that she will discover new and captivating performances to enjoy. She values her friendships and often attends cultural events with friends who share her interests. Despite her frustrations, Evelyn remains open-minded and continues to seek out new experiences and opportunities for intellectual stimulation.

    Social Media Usage: Evelyn uses Instagram to stay in touch with friends and family, share updates, and engage in literary discussions. While she appreciates its convenience, she prioritizes face-to-face interactions and finds excessive social media time draining.

    Motivations: Exploring cultural activities, staying connected with friends and family, lifelong learning. Now that her kids have grown and left home, Evelyn and her husband find themself with new free time that they would like to spend in cultural and enriching activities they enjoy.

    Goals: Engaging in leisure activities, attending cultural events, such as ballet, shows and concerts, but also expanding her knowledge.

    Frustrations: Feeling that theatre productions are becoming less appealing over time, challenges with crowded theatre, limited availability of high-quality performances

Brand Name

    Why The Backstage?: We chose “The Backstage” for our Brand’s name, because in theatres even if the show is on the stage there is a lot of hard work going on in the backstage that the audience does not get to experience. Therefore, we want to bring the backstage to the audience, where people can embrace our brand with an open mind and honesty, being left with the feeling that they are being cared for in a supportive environment. This way, we are able to expand the theatre experience, without secrets, just authenticity.

Visual Identity

    We understand our persona's frustrations with the declining quality of theatre productions and the challenges of crowded venues. That's why our visual identity is designed to create a welcoming and immersive atmosphere, encouraging her and other theatre lovers to rediscover the joy of attending cultural events. Through our brand's commitment to authenticity and refinement, we aim to provide them with memorable experiences that align with their motivations for exploration and lifelong learning.

    The colours: Our visual identity, characterized by the calming tons of blue, purple, and the vibrant energy of green, is carefully made to align with our brand values and vision. Blue conveys a sense of trust and honesty, aligning perfectly with our commitment to integrity and sincerity. Purple symbolizes openness and creativity, reflecting our value of open-mindedness and our belief in the power of diverse perspectives within the theatrical community. Green represents growth, renewal, and care, embodying our dedication to raise connections and supporting one another in a world where authentic relationships are increasingly rare.

    The fonts: Furthermore, the chosen fonts—The Seasons, Bely Display, Bennet Banner, and DM Sans Light 300—enhance the sophistication and elegance of our brand, reinforcing the idea of revealing oneself "backstage" in a context that is both authentic and refined. By combining these elements, our visual identity creates a cohesive and immersive experience for our target audience of theatre lovers. It invites them to not only engage with the performances on stage but also to connect with each other and with themselves on a deeper level, in line with our brand vision of fostering a caring world of genuine connections. In essence, our visual identity serves as a visual manifestation of our brand values and vision, providing a compelling and resonant platform for meaningful engagement within the theatrical community.

    First Lean Canvas

    Problem

        • Limited availability: Not all performances are accessible to everyone due to scheduling conflicts, geographical limitations, or sold-out shows.

        • Existing alternatives:
          • Theatres in the Netherlands offer live streams or recordings of their performances for audiences unable to attend in person. Some theatres also expand the run of an event to accommodate more audience members.

      1. Struggle to find tickets: Nowadays people do not have the patience to look through multiple websites to find the right tickets.

        • Existing alternatives: Platforms like Ticket swap provide users to search available tickets for different events in one place.

      2. High ticket prices: Theatre tickets can be expensive, making it inaccessible for some individuals.

        • Existing alternatives: Some theatres in the Netherlands offer discounted ticket programs for students, seniors, or members of specific organizations. Along with, theatres that may offer rush tickets or last-minute deals for unsold seats shortly before a performance.

    Solution

  • Centralized Ticketing Platform: that combines listings from various ticket venues. Users can search for tickets using filters such as location, date, genre, and price range, simplifying the process of finding the right tickets.
  • Benefits: Our website provides Convenience and Efficiency. This audience likes when the information is not spread around. They want things to be centralized while easy and quick to understand. Users can now benefit from our website and save time and effort, because they can now easily find and purchase tickets for their favorite events all in one place.

  • Membership Programs: Introduce membership programs that provide exclusive access to pre-sales, discounts, and priority seating for members. This rewards loyal customers and incentivizes them to purchase tickets through the website.
  • Benefits: These exclusive features will make users feel valued and special, setting "The Backstage" apart as a platform that prioritizes customer satisfaction and engagement.

  • Price Comparison Tools: Incorporate price comparison tools that allow users to compare ticket prices across different platforms.
  • Benefits: This feature sets "The Backstage" apart by providing users with transparency and the ability to make informed decisions. Along with that users can now find the best deals and save time by avoiding manual comparison shopping.

    Second Lean Canvas

    Problem

      1. Limited Access to Reviews and Opinions:Many theatre enthusiasts struggle to find reliable and comprehensive reviews of plays and performances.

        • Existing alternatives:
        • Websites like Cultuurpodia.nl and Theaterkrant.nl provide comprehensive reviews and opinions on shows across the Netherlands.
        • Newspapers like De Volkskrant and NRC Handelsblad feature reviews of theater performances, providing readers with expert critiques and recommendations on what to see in the Netherlands.
        • Social Media Groups: Facebook groups and Twitter accounts like @TheaterNL offer platforms for Dutch theater enthusiasts to share opinions. Many Dutch cities also have local theater communities and forums where enthusiasts can engage in discussions about performances happening in their area.

      2. Lack of Community Engagement:Theatre lovers often crave opportunities to connect with others who share their passion for the world of the performances.

        • Existing alternatives:
        • Platforms like Forum voor Theater serve as online hubs for theater enthusiasts to discuss performances.
        • Subscription services like Theaterclub.nl offer discounted tickets to theater performances and exclusive events for members, fostering a sense of community among subscribers who share a love for the live shows.
        • Facebook groups dedicated to theatre, is currently on option for enthusiasts to engage with each other.
        • Websites like Meetup.nl host theater-related meetup groups where enthusiasts can connect with like-minded individuals.

      3. Difficulty in Discovering New Shows: From the interviews we found out that sometimes people don’t really know what is available for them in the theatre or do not have the patience to look for it.

        • Existing alternatives: Platforms like Theater.nl and de Uitagenda showcase a variety of shows across the Netherlands. Municipalities often have cultural websites like UITagenda Amsterdam or Uitagenda Rotterdam, which feature upcoming theatre events and performances in their respective cities, helping locals and visitors alike discover new shows nearby.
        • Events like the Nederlandse Theater Festival and the Amsterdam Fringe Festival, providi opportunities for audiences to discover new and innovative productions in a festival atmosphere.
        • Subscribing to services like Podium Cadeaukaart and CJP (Cultuurkaart) not only offers discounts on tickets but also provides access to an organised selection of theater shows.

  • Limited Access to Reviews and Opinions: Our website will provide a feature (reviews) where users can access a wide range of reviews and opinions from other theatre lovers, helping them make informed decisions about which shows to attend along with other thoughts about those plays/performances.
  • Benefits: Users can confidently select shows that align with their preferences and expectations, which increases their satisfaction. They won’t need look around in different places for reviews (which can be overwhelming and confusing), as our platform offers a variety of insights from a diverse community of theatre enthusiasts.

  • Lack of Community Engagement: Our website serves as a dynamic hub for fostering community interaction and engagement among theatre lovers. Besides of the reviews feature where people can share their thoughts and opinions about the plays and performances they have seen. We will have exclusive chat feature where people can connect and create groups which will facilitate communication and interaction among users creating a vibrant community where members can discuss their favourite plays, share recommendations, and exchange insights about what is going on in the theatre world.
  • Benefits: By facilitating connections within the theatre community, our platform enriches the overall theatre-going experience for users. They can develop new friendships, discover shared interests, and stay informed about upcoming events and developments in the theatre world.

  • Difficulty in Discovering New Shows: Our website addresses this challenge by providing an organized list of upcoming programmes linked to each user's location and preferences. This feature eliminates the need for users to search for shows themselves, offering them easy accessibility to a diverse range of available performances.
  • Benefits: Users can effortlessly discover new and exciting shows that they might otherwise miss out on, expanding their cultural horizons and enriching their theatre-going experiences. By simplifying the process of finding new shows, our website saves users time and effort, allowing them to focus on enjoying the theatre.

    Problem

      1. Limited Access to Reviews and Opinions:Many theatre enthusiasts struggle to find reliable and comprehensive reviews of plays and performances.

        • Existing alternatives:
        • Websites like Cultuurpodia.nl and Theaterkrant.nl provide comprehensive reviews and opinions on shows across the Netherlands.
        • Newspapers like De Volkskrant and NRC Handelsblad feature reviews of theater performances, providing readers with expert critiques and recommendations on what to see in the Netherlands.
        • Social Media Groups: Facebook groups and Twitter accounts like @TheaterNL offer platforms for Dutch theater enthusiasts to share opinions. Many Dutch cities also have local theater communities and forums where enthusiasts can engage in discussions about performances happening in their area.

      2. Lack of Community Engagement:Theatre lovers often crave opportunities to connect with others who share their passion for the world of the performances.

        • Existing alternatives:
        • Platforms like Forum voor Theater serve as online hubs for theater enthusiasts to discuss performances.
        • Subscription services like Theaterclub.nl offer discounted tickets to theater performances and exclusive events for members, fostering a sense of community among subscribers who share a love for the live shows.
        • Facebook groups dedicated to theatre, is currently on option for enthusiasts to engage with each other.
        • Websites like Meetup.nl host theater-related meetup groups where enthusiasts can connect with like-minded individuals.

      3. Difficulty in Discovering New Shows:From the interviews we found out that sometimes people don’t really know what is available for them in the theatre or do not have the patience to look for it.

        • Existing alternatives: Platforms like Theater.nl and de Uitagenda showcase a variety of shows across the Netherlands. Municipalities often have cultural websites like UITagenda Amsterdam or Uitagenda Rotterdam, which feature upcoming theatre events and performances in their respective cities, helping locals and visitors alike discover new shows nearby.
        • Events like the Nederlandse Theater Festival and the Amsterdam Fringe Festival, provide opportunities for audiences to discover new and innovative productions in a festival atmosphere.
        • Subscribing to services like Podium Cadeaukaart and CJP (Cultuurkaart) not only offers discounts on tickets but also provides access to an organised selection of theater shows.

      4. Difficulty Navigating Complex Websites: Many theatre enthusiasts aged 50 to 65 encounter challenges when navigating online platforms due to small fonts, cluttered layouts, and complex navigation menus. Existing theatre websites may not be optimized for older users, leading to frustration and difficulty in accessing important information such as show reviews, community forums, and ticket bookings.

        • Existing alternatives: The reach of Dutch organizations dedicated to help older adults improve their digital skills such as SeniorWeb and ANBO Digital Support. They offer online courses, workshops, and resources specifically aimed at teaching seniors how to navigate complex websites.
