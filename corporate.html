Content

Vision

    Problem/context: In this digital Era people's connections are becoming superficial and shallow

    Statement/belief: It is necessary to drift away from an excessive self-focus and open our minds and hearts to others

    Desired Outcome: Only then we can revive the sense of community and togetherness and build a world of real connections

    “In a world where life gets busy, and connections become brief and shallow. We believe that only if we manage to look outside of ourselves to others. Only then we can build a caring world of real connections, where we can truly reveal ourselves backstage."

Values

  1. Honesty: Our brand stands for authenticity and comfort, and we can only reach that outcome with honesty. By prioritizing honesty, we are motivating people to engage in deeper and more genuine connections.
  2. Open-mindedness: We aim to create a safe environment, where everyone feels welcome regardless of their background, age, gender etc It is important that we stablish the bases needed for people to feel seen, so that they can share their sincere opinion without being or feeling judged.
  3. Care: We prioritise empathy and compassion for others. This value involves looking beyond ourselves to others and build meaningful connections, fostering a community where individuals can be authentic and reveal themselves and feel supported by sharing their opinions and thoughts for a common passion.
  4. Support: We aim to create a platform where people can be themselves and talk to others about their connection with theatre. It is important to have a sense of support within this community where everyone can share their experiences without being judged.

Persona

    Name: Evelyn

    Age: 56 years old

    Occupation: Librarian

    Education: Bachelor's degree in English Literature

    Personality: Friendly, curious, sociable, cat-person

    Marital Status: Married

    Bio: Evelyn works as a librarian for most of her career, fostering a love of literature and learning in her community. In her free time, she attends book club meetings and explores cultural events like theatre performances. Evelyn has noticed a decline in the quality of theatre productions in recent years, which has decreased her enthusiasm for attending shows. However, she remains hopeful that she will discover new and captivating performances to enjoy. She values her friendships and often attends cultural events with friends who share her interests. Despite her frustrations, Evelyn remains open-minded and continues to seek out new experiences and opportunities for intellectual stimulation.

    Social Media Usage: Evelyn uses Instagram to stay in touch with friends and family, share updates, and engage in literary discussions. While she appreciates its convenience, she prioritizes face-to-face interactions and finds excessive social media time draining.

    Motivations: Exploring cultural activities, staying connected with friends and family, lifelong learning. Now that her kids have grown and left home, Evelyn and her husband find themself with new free time that they would like to spend in cultural and enriching activities they enjoy.

    Goals: Engaging in leisure activities, attending cultural events, such as ballet, shows and concerts, but also expanding her knowledge.

    Frustrations: Feeling that theatre productions are becoming less appealing over time, challenges with crowded theatre, limited availability of high-quality performances

Brand Name

    Why The Backstage?: We chose “The Backstage” for our Brand’s name, because in theatres even if the show is on the stage there is a lot of hard work going on in the backstage that the audience does not get to experience. Therefore, we want to bring the backstage to the audience, where people can embrace our brand with an open mind and honesty, being left with the feeling that they are being cared for in a supportive environment. This way, we are able to expand the theatre experience, without secrets, just authenticity.

Visual Identity

    We understand our persona's frustrations with the declining quality of theatre productions and the challenges of crowded venues. That's why our visual identity is designed to create a welcoming and immersive atmosphere, encouraging her and other theatre lovers to rediscover the joy of attending cultural events. Through our brand's commitment to authenticity and refinement, we aim to provide them with memorable experiences that align with their motivations for exploration and lifelong learning.

    The colours: Our visual identity, characterized by the calming tons of blue, purple, and the vibrant energy of green, is carefully made to align with our brand values and vision. Blue conveys a sense of trust and honesty, aligning perfectly with our commitment to integrity and sincerity. Purple symbolizes openness and creativity, reflecting our value of open-mindedness and our belief in the power of diverse perspectives within the theatrical community. Green represents growth, renewal, and care, embodying our dedication to raise connections and supporting one another in a world where authentic relationships are increasingly rare.

    The fonts: Furthermore, the chosen fonts—The Seasons, Bely Display, Bennet Banner and DM Sans Light 300—enhance the sophistication and elegance of our brand, reinforcing the idea of revealing oneself "backstage" in a context that is both authentic and refined. Since our target audience is also older people, we decided to use understandable (easy to read) fonts, that are also big and well visible. By combining these elements, our visual identity creates a cohesive and immersive experience for our target audience of theatre lovers. It invites them to not only engage with the performances on stage but also to connect with each other and with themselves on a deeper level, in line with our brand vision of fostering a caring world of genuine connections. In essence, our visual identity serves as a visual manifestation of our brand values and vision, providing a compelling and resonant platform for meaningful engagement within the theatrical community.

    Context of campaign and promotional activities

    This marketing campaign has the purpose to raise awareness and establish solid relationships within a brand and its potential users. The brand in question is our ‘The Backstage’, where we aim to be put out our product (website) in the market by Friday 5th of April. We tried to engage our target audience by sharing interesting content both on mail and social media. It was important for us that our content portrays the values and intentions of the project. In order to accomplish the goal, we previously had to come across a crystal-clear definition of our value proposition. We defined The Backstage as ‘a brand that intends to connect theatre lovers in an honest way; through care and support, it aims to create a safe and modern space in which users can express themselves and learn from each other open-mindedly'. A message that includes our four essential values: care, support, honesty and open mindedness.

    The promotional activities we executed were taken to term on two media channels. We used e-mail and Instagram to make our brand (and its consequent message) come across. Despite having both fronts open, we took the decision of dedicating extra effort and time in the latest channel (Instagram) since this was our main mean of communication. We chose Instagram because it is a simple and dynamic platform, very useful to spread our word. Anyone can see what is published in a public profile on this social media, which means your content can pop up in anyone's for your page. Therefore, it seems easier dragging new people and reaching a greater number of viewers. Furthermore, Instagram is vastly known and very popular, so it just made sense to use it. But also, Instagram is very visual and fast, creating brief impressions on people which are crucial to build our brand awareness

    Types of posts we made:

  • Event announcements (website launch, newsletter launch) - With these posts we wanted to keep the audience informed of what we just dropped or what we had yet to come. Brand objective of raising awareness about ‘The Backstage’
  • Brand Insights - the purpose of this posts is for the audience to feel closer to our brand. They get to know what we are all about. We stablish strong relationships with them by sharing our intentions.
  • Behind the scenes – these types of posts show transparency and authenticity we do not want our audience that we are hiding anything from them
  • Promotional video – raise awareness and reach more people (meet our other objective of gaining followers). Make them feel attached to the company and start building a relationship
  • Survey/polls, questions - with these posts we intended to make our profile dynamic and interactive.
  • Memes, fun facts, industry news - aimed to catch people’s attention, make them have fun and be invested in our page.
  • Good morning and good night posts, thank you posts, greetings- to keep the audience engaged and transmit the values of caring and support that our brand stands for
    • On top of that, we also used Chimp mail to create and distribute our newsletter. We decided to publish a new article on our newsletter every Sunday and keep our audience informed about it with our Instagram posts (we added the link to the newsletter in our Instagram bio too). We decided to create a newsletter to make our audience feel closer to us, curious, attached and consequently become loyal supporters of our brand. All in all, the intention of the newsletter was to create a subscriber base and stablish strong long-term relationships with them.

    Target Audience

    The audience chosen was adults within 50 and 65 years old. As we did our first interviews and dived deep into the Theatre Lovers we wanted to target. We decided on this age group due to the Google forms we created because most results were between the ages 45 to 54. However, in the in-person interviews, we found older people (between 55 and 77) who were interested in the theatre. With that said we decided to make our target group around the age gap (50 to 65), so it would be extremely broad. The generation targeted is more present on Facebook. However, several business and brand profiles are being put down and banned on Facebook, so our main social media platform during the campaign will be Instagram, due to being equally valid option for meeting our purpose.

    Effect

    We want our target audience to feel a sense of community, where than can easily connect with other and freely share their passions and opinions about theatre and well-informed. We aim to guarantee that the before and after theatre experience is more enjoyable and easier. Moreover, we want to build trust, meaning in the target audience’s eyes we are a trustworthy brand.

    Learning Points

    Reflecting on the Communication & Media Plan executed for 'The Backstage' marketing campaign, several key learning points were recognised by the team from the challenges faced during the project.

    One significant issue that surfaced was the lack of commitment from certain team members, leading to difficulties not only in the workflow, following the content calendar, but also lack of good communication within the team. This lack of consistency slowed down our progress and kept us from working on perfecting this project. We tried to solve it with discussions and by setting inner deadlines but most of the time it was unsuccessful, we think that perhaps, in the beginning, we should’ve defined roles internally so the team itself could be more organized and know exactly what their responsibilities were.

    Another key learning point we encountered was the difficulty we had to find our target audience, as international students we do not have a lot of contact with adults in the ages of our target group in the Netherlands. We tried to interview people in the theatre in Breda and around town but did not get the best of outcomes, most people would tell us they were not simply keen on theatre. We were advised to go to different cities, but during the first weeks of this project we did not manage to organize such option and we also tried to avoid having extra costs. Nonetheless, it would have been certainly helpful and make our progress to find more people easier.

    Additionally, while our campaign objectives were ambitious, they may have been somewhat unrealistic given the timeframe and resources available. Specifically, we fell short on our targets for Instagram followers (which according to our objectives were 150, but we end up with a total of 77) and number of reaches (we expected 500 reaches, however we end up with 221). This shortfall highlights the need for more realistic goal setting and detailed understanding of the campaign's potential impact. Another aspect worth noting is the decision to focus solely on Instagram. We firstly wanted to also use a Facebook page, but we discover the platform has been banning business pages, so instead of trying to find a solution we sticked to only one channel. However, we are sure that diversifying our platform usage could have expanded our audience reach and engagement opportunities. We could have been more dynamic in our posts, try to invest more on videos and reels (since those showed up to be one of our user's favourites formats of content). We should have also search for more feedback from our users by doing surveys, polls or Q&A's, which would have been helpful to understand not only what we were doing well but where we were failing at.

    Additionally, we published 4 newsletters every Sunday during our campaign, we were disappointed to notice that not only we did not achieve our 20 subscribers' goal (which was 10), as well as, only having 40% of them opening and engaging with our newsletters. We should examine the content, format, and timing of our newsletters to identify areas for improvement. Moreover, we should also improve our newsletters’ advertising, during the campaign we posted about our newsletter twice a week. However, we believe that perhaps sharing it in WhatsApp groups within our target audience and even do offline marketing such as making QR code flyers and spread it around the cities or around theatres in the Netherlands would have helped in a better outcome.

    Overall, we notice that along the campaign there was a decrease of interactions and audience engagement and that somehow, discouraged our team, which we now understand that it should have had the opposite effect and make us work harder and get more invested. However, in our opinion in the beginning we reached a lot of people and were invested, through mouth-of-mouth marketing we spoke to our family and friends and shared with them a google forms created by us that end up having around 115 answers, which we thought it was a good number. For that reason, we conclude that even if the campaign faced challenges and did not fully meet its objectives, it offered us valuable lessons for future campaigns, including the importance of team commitment and investment, realistic goal setting, platform diversification, audience engagement, and continuous evaluation and adaptation.

    Future Planning

    In the coming year, we should focus on diversifying our content strategy even further. Since, our current mix of content has not been as engaging as we wanted, having a noticeable decrease in reaches and interactions, in the last weeks of campaign, we wonder if exploring additional ways of entertainment could deepen audience engagement.

    For instance, incorporating user-generated content such as Instagram lives and partnerships with performers and influencers could empower our community to share their theater experiences as well, fostering a sense of inclusivity and participation. Additionally, interactive quizzes or games related to theater trivia as it was executed in our campaign. However, with more frequency and only through Instagram stories or reels format (since according to Instagram insights this type of content format showed up to be the most popular and liked between our followers) which will introduce the element of fun and encourage more audience interaction (just as how we wanted to have happen in this previous month).

    Moreover, enhancing personalization will be crucial in strengthening our connection with the audience. By segmenting our audience, this means target our audience within the Netherlands, if we narrow it down to a location, we have more time to explore the Dutch events and shows instead of creating content that is very broad and not reachable, for example if we post theatre news about Spain it will not be very realistic to have our Dutch audience engaged in that content as much as if it was about theatre in the Netherlands.

    While Instagram has been our primary platform, we will try to go over Facebook (that is now banning business pages) and understand how we could implement our content in there (since it is one of the most used platforms within our target audience). As social media consumers ourselves, we spoke as a team and realised how the number of adults on Tik Tok has been increasing. We are now sure that for a future we should not just focus in one social media but expand and diversifying our media strategy, because every platform offers unique opportunities for content distribution and community building which will be a must to broaden our reach and engage new audience segments. Furthermore, establishing partnerships and trends such as “account takeover” (where the influencer posts the whole day and engages with people through Lives, Surveys, and Q&A’s) with influencers or micro-influencers in the theater niche can amplify our brand's reach and credibility.

    For the upcoming year we will pay more attention and keep track/analyse the performance of our campaign elements such as looking through Instagram insights weekly. This way we can gain valuable insights into audience behaviour and content effectiveness.

    Maintaining open communication channels with our audience will be essential for gathering feedback and insights as well. Regular surveys, polls, or Q&A sessions can help us understand audience preferences, allowing us to improve our content strategy.

    Additionally, we would like to host offline events (for example at universities, theatres, and cafes) to reach new audiences. Creating a realistic marketing budget towards targeted ads on social media platforms which might amplify our brand's visibility.

    In summary, by implementing these recommendations and reinforce a bigger commitment to innovation and audience engagement, we can bring 'The Backstage' forward and be seen as a truly trusted and vibrant community for theater enthusiasts.
